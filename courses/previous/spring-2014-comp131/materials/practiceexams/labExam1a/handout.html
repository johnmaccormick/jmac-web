For the calculateWindchill method, the windchill is calculated from the temperature and wind speed. In the following table, each column represents a range of temperatures (in degrees Fahrenheit), and each row represents a range of wind speeds (in miles per hour). The table entries contain the number that should be subtracted from the temperature to compute the windchill. For example, if the temperature is 27 degrees and the wind speed is 16 MPH, the corresponding table entry is 10 and so the windchill is 17 degrees (27 - 10). If the temperature is 12 degrees and the wind speed is 35 MPH, the windchill is -8 degrees (12 - 20).
