Getting Started with BlueJ
We will frequently use code provided by the authors of your text, which can be found on the CD that came with your book. To work with it, you need to copy it from the CD into your network account.

To copy the code from your CD:

  • Put the disc from the book into the CD drive (located on the right side of the monitor), with the label side facing you.

  • Open the Finder (the blue smiley-face icon on your desktop taskbar).

  • In the Finder window select your "private" folder, which should be listed in the left pane.

  • Open the "CS131" folder your created in Lab 01, or create the folder now if you did Lab 01 using your partner's account.

  • Create a new folder called "book" inside your "CS131" folder. (You can do this by selecting "New Folder" from the Finder's File menu.)

  • Now double-click the CD icon on your desktop to open it.

  • Navigate to the Projects folder in the CD's Finder window.

  • Copy chapter01 through chapter04 from the CD to your new book folder. (You can do this by selecting the desired folders and dragging them to their new location.)

  • Remove the CD. To do this, "right-click" (ie, hold the control key down while clicking with the only mouse button you have), and select Eject Objects-First.

To open the BlueJ programming environment:

  • Click on the BlueJ icon on the desktop taskbar.

  • A dialog will open, asking whether you really want to open BlueJ even though it was downloaded from the Internet. Click Open.

To open the project you will work with today:

  • From the BlueJ menu, select Project > Open Project.
  • Navigate to your CS131/book/chapter01 folder.
  • Select figures.

You are now ready to begin working through the text. Please spend the next part of class reading Sections 1.1 through Section 1.6 and following the directions there. Notice that there are some instructions embedded in the regular text and others that are offset and labelled "Exercises." You should do all of these activities.
