Optional cultural events

Several cultural events are being offered as optional components of the SCIE258 course. While not directly related to the theme of our course, these events will contribute to the more general goals of global study through cultural understanding. The events are optional and will have no effect on your course grade.

See below for details about the individual events. Dickinson will be paying half the cost of the tickets and you will be responsible for the other half of the cost for each event you choose to attend. Students taking the humanities course are also welcome to sign up for these events on the sign-up sheet linked below (Dickinson will pay 50% of the cost for humanities students too).

By Wednesday, July 5, please indicate which events you would like to attend on the following sign-up sheet:

  • Cultural events sign-up sheet

Event details:

  • Prom Concert at the Royal Albert Hall at 7:30 PM on Tuesday, September 5. Tickets cost 15 pounds so the cost to you is 7.5 pounds. This is in a spectacular venue with a great program of 20th-century classical music including a Bartok piano concerto and a Dvorak Symphony.
  • Shakespeare's King Lear at the Globe theatre, 7:30 PM on Monday, September 11. Tickets are 5 pounds so the cost to you is 2.5 pounds. Shakespeare's Globe is a reconstruction of the playhouse built and used by Shakespeare's own theater company. We will be standing in the open-air "yard" area, watching the play on foot the same way most of the audience would have in Shakespeare's time. If it rains, we will get wet!
  • Gate, a new play by Artemis Fitzalan Howard at the Cockpit Theater, Thursday, September 14 at 7:30 PM. Tickets are 18 pounds so the cost to you is 9 pounds. The play sounds like it should be very interesting for our group, since it challenges some of our society's ideas about self-interest and scientific certainty.
